## Open Questions: Resolved and Outstanding

### RESOLVED — OQ-1: Gusto pricing discrepancy ($40 vs $49 base)
**Resolution:** Gusto's official pricing page confirms **Simple plan at $49 base + $6/employee/month**. The Eagle Rock CFO source citing $40 was outdated. Stinger-forge should use the $49 figure. Also note Gusto now offers a 4-tier structure (Contractor Only / Simple / Plus / Premium), with Premium at $180 base + $22/employee for white-glove HR.

### RESOLVED — OQ-2: Rippling modular pricing
**Resolution:** Rippling does NOT publish public pricing. All pricing is custom-quoted via sales call. The "$8 base + add-ons" claim cited by practitioner sources is a rough approximation. **Stinger-forge should frame Rippling as "custom-quoted; expect $20-$30/employee/month for HR+Payroll, with IT and Finance modules adding to that."** Critically: qualified venture-backed startups can get 6 free months of Rippling Payroll/HR/IT/Finance.

### PARTIALLY RESOLVED — OQ-3: Deel-Carta native integration status
**Resolution:** Carta's Connectors Directory still does not explicitly list Deel as a direct integration. However, Carta's 2026 MCP/Plugins ecosystem (April 2026 launch) creates a new integration pathway via AI agents that can query Carta data, potentially bridging the Deel-Carta gap. **Stinger-forge should:** (1) document the current native-integration gap in `guides/05-carta-handoff.md`, (2) note that Finch middleware may bridge Deel-Carta, (3) flag that Carta MCP is a forward-looking option for AI-native teams.

### OUTSTANDING — OQ-4: 2026 PFML new state programs (3 new programs launching)
The multi-state compliance source mentions "three new Paid Family and Medical Leave programs launching in 2026" but does not identify which three states. Known launch: Minnesota (2026). **Stinger-forge should conduct a follow-up search to identify the other two states and their mandatory payroll deduction rates** for `guides/06-compliance-hotspots.md`. Likely candidates: Maine (passed 2023, programmed launch 2026) and Delaware (passed 2022, programmed launch 2026).

### RESOLVED — OQ-5: Oyster pricing ($499 vs $599 vs $699)
**Resolution:** Oyster's official pricing page confirms **EOR at $699/employee/month** (annual discounts available). The $499/$599 figures cited in comparison articles are outdated or refer to the "Oyster for Startups" discount program (save up to $1,200). Stinger-forge should use $699 as the headline EOR price for Oyster, with $499-$599 effective pricing applicable only through the Oyster for Startups program.

## NEW Findings From Official Vendor Pages (Not in Original Plan)

### 1. Justworks restructured pricing (previously $99 Plus → now $79 Basic / $109 Plus)
The PeopleOpsClub comparison article cited Justworks Plus at $99/employee. Justworks' official page now shows **PEO Basic at $79 and PEO Plus at $109**, plus a new standalone **Payroll tier at $8/employee + $50 base**. The Gusto vs Justworks cost comparison in `guides/01-platform-selection.md` must use the new figures.

### 2. Remote MCP — AI agent infrastructure (2026)
Remote launched Remote MCP allowing AI agents to connect to live payroll/contracts/compliance/org data without API keys. Meaningful differentiator for AI-native teams.

### 3. Remote annual billing discount
Remote EOR is $699/month monthly billing or $599/month annual billing (14% discount). The earlier comparison sources only cited $599 — they were implicitly quoting the annual rate.

### 4. Remote "no deposit required" — contradicts earlier source
The GlobalSolo comparison article claimed Remote requires "1 month of gross salary deposit per employee." Remote's official statement contradicts this: "No deposits or accruals required (rare exceptions for high-risk)." Stinger-forge should resolve this contradiction by treating the deposit as situational rather than universal.

### 5. Carta Law (May 13, 2026) and Carta MCP/Plugins (April 2026)
Carta acquired Avantia Law (AI-native PE law firm), rebranded as Carta Law. Carta MCP listed on Anthropic Connectors Directory. Three Claude Plugins launched: Cap Table, Investors, CRM. Major AI-era ecosystem expansion.

### 6. Oyster Shell — $500K misclassification protection
Oyster offers a contractor-misclassification insurance product up to $500,000 coverage that is unique among the EOR platforms compared. Meaningful for `guides/02-classification-matrix.md` risk-mitigation section.

### 7. Deel public pricing transparency
Deel publishes full pricing for EOR, US PEO, Contractors, COR, HR modules, Managed Payroll, IT, and Talent. This transparency is itself a procurement-driven selection factor against Rippling's opaque quote-only model.
