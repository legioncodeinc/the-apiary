## Section 2: IRS 3-category test

### Behavioral control (check all that apply)

- [ ] Company controls HOW the worker performs the work (step-by-step instructions)
- [ ] Company controls WHEN the worker works (set schedule/hours)
- [ ] Company controls WHERE the worker works (required on-site or specific location)
- [ ] Company provides training on methods/procedures
- [ ] Worker follows company-set processes and workflows

**Behavioral control score:** ___ of 5 (3+ = strong employee indicator)

### Financial control (check all that apply)

- [ ] Worker has no significant personal investment in tools/equipment (company provides all)
- [ ] Worker does not advertise or offer services to the general public
- [ ] Worker cannot realize a profit or loss from the engagement
- [ ] Worker is paid regular wages (vs. per-project flat fee)
- [ ] Worker has no unreimbursed business expenses

**Financial control score:** ___ of 5 (3+ = strong employee indicator)

### Type of relationship (check all that apply)

- [ ] Employment contract or employee benefits exist (health, 401k, vacation)
- [ ] Relationship is indefinite (not for a specific project or time period)
- [ ] Worker's services are integral to the company's regular business
- [ ] Company has the right to discharge the worker at will
- [ ] Both parties perceive the relationship as employment

**Relationship score:** ___ of 5 (3+ = strong employee indicator)

**IRS total score:** ___ of 15 (9+ = strong employee indicator; seek legal advice at 6-8)

## Section 3: California AB5 (if worker is in California)

| Prong | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|---|---|
| A — Free from control | Is the worker free from the company's control in performing the work? | |
| B — Outside usual course | Does the worker perform work OUTSIDE the usual course of the company's business? | |
| C — Independent business | Is the worker customarily engaged in an independently established trade/business? | |

**All three prongs must pass for contractor classification in California.**

If Prong B fails (worker performs work integral to the company's core business): W-2 required in California.
