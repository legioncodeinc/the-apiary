# Example: Platform Selection at Seed Stage

A worked example of a seed-stage founder choosing between Carta and Pulley in 2026.

Demonstrates: `guides/01-platform-selection.md`

---

## Scenario

Two founders have just incorporated a Delaware C-Corp. They have:
- 10,000,000 shares split 50/50 between founders.
- One SAFE for $200K at a $3M post-money cap from a solo bee.
- A plan to raise a $1.5M pre-seed round (SAFE) from a pre-seed fund.
- 3 employees planned in the next 6 months who will receive options.
- Investor: solo bee who is not affiliated with a VC fund and has no preference for either platform.

---

## Evaluation

### Carta

- **Pricing:** Carta Launch (free, up to 25 stakeholders). After adding the first 3 option grantees, the company will still be under 25 stakeholders and could stay on the free tier through the pre-seed round.
- **Features needed at this stage:** Cap table recording, SAFE recording, option grants, 409A (will be needed before first grant).
- **Investor ecosystem:** Bee has no preference; pre-seed fund not yet confirmed.
- **UX:** More complex than Pulley; onboarding takes longer.
- **409A integration:** Available; pricing approximately $1,500-$3,000 for a seed-stage company.

### Pulley

- **Pricing:** Pulley free tier (more generous limits than Carta Launch). Startup plan (approximately $600/year) covers early-stage features including round modeling.
- **Features needed at this stage:** Same as Carta. Pulley's round modeling tools are better at this stage for founders planning the pre-seed SAFE.
- **Investor ecosystem:** Pre-seed fund not yet confirmed; most seed-stage investors work with either platform.
- **UX:** Cleaner; faster onboarding; founders report faster data entry and scenario modeling.
- **409A integration:** Available via third-party partner; slightly less seamless than Carta's integrated service.

---

## Recommendation

**Pulley** at this stage, for the following reasons:

1. **Cost:** Equivalent or lower than Carta for the features needed.
2. **UX:** More intuitive for the founders to self-manage without hiring a CFO or cap table specialist.
3. **Round modeling:** The pre-seed round modeling tools are meaningfully better, which matters as the founders are actively modeling the pre-seed raise.
4. **No investor forcing function:** Without a Carta-first VC on the cap table yet, there is no reason to pay for Carta's ecosystem integration.

**When to reconsider Carta:** When the lead Series A investor uses Carta's investor portal, or when the company needs the integrated 409A service and a third-party provider is adding friction. At that point, migration from Pulley to Carta is manageable with Pulley's export tools and Carta's import process.

---

## Note for the future

At Series A, many institutional lead investors (especially larger funds) use Carta's investor portal to manage their portfolio. When the lead Series A investor is identified, ask them: "Do you have a preference for cap table platform?" If they say Carta, migrate at or before the round close, when the cap table is being restated anyway.
