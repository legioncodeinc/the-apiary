# Anthropic Contextual Retrieval

**Source:** Anthropic blog — https://www.anthropic.com/news/contextual-retrieval (2024)
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-25
**Status:** Informational — discussed in `guides/07-knowledge-base.md` for context. NOT currently active in the deploying product.
**Numbers tag:** vendor-directional (35–50% retrieval-failure reduction is Anthropic's claim; reproduce on your corpus before adopting).

---

## TL;DR

Contextual retrieval prepends a small (50–100 token) chunk-specific context (generated by an LLM at ingestion) to each chunk before embedding. Anthropic reports 35–50% reduction in retrieval failure on long structured documents.

---

## How it works

For each chunk:

1. Generate a brief summary of the chunk's role within the parent document (LLM call at ingestion time).
2. Prepend that summary to the chunk text BEFORE embedding.
3. Index the contextualized chunk into the vector DB.

```
Original chunk:
"In this case, the patient was administered 200mg of the active ingredient over 6 hours."

Contextualized chunk:
"This passage is from Section 4.2 of the 2024 clinical trial report on drug X. In this case,
 the patient was administered 200mg of the active ingredient over 6 hours."
```

The contextualized chunk has a richer embedding because it carries parent-doc context.

---

## When it pays off

- **Long, structured documents where chunks lose parent context** — legal filings, medical records, financial reports.
- **Compute budget allows** an LLM call per chunk at ingestion (one-time cost).
- **Eval shows the lift** on your corpus.

---

## When it doesn't

- **Short documents** where each chunk is already self-contained.
- **Tight LLM-cost budget** — the contextualization step adds a model call per chunk at ingestion.
- **Retrieval already healthy** at vector + rerank without contextualization.

---

## the deploying product's current stance

**Not adopted.** the deploying product's `chunkText()` already prepends the document title to each chunk:

```typescript
const chunk = `${title}\n${rawChunk}`;
```

This is a lighter version of contextual retrieval — title-as-context, not LLM-generated-context. Sufficient for the deploying product's coaching corpora (which are well-structured methodology docs, not multi-section legal filings).

A push to adopt full contextual retrieval would require:

1. Eval baseline at current state.
2. Implement contextualization at ingestion (modify `indexKnowledgeDocument()`).
3. Re-index a sample of the corpus.
4. Compare retrieval precision.
5. Adopt if lift > 5% and ingestion cost is acceptable.

---

## Stacks with fixed-size chunking

Contextual retrieval is **not in conflict** with the Vectara NAACL 2025 finding (`research/2026-04-25-vectara-naacl-2025-chunking.md`). The Vectara finding is about chunk-METHOD (fixed-size beats semantic). Contextual retrieval is about chunk-AUGMENTATION (prepend context). Both can apply: fixed-size chunking + contextual augmentation.
