# Guide 07: Growth Benchmarks

2026 per-platform engagement rate benchmarks, follower growth trajectories, and realistic 90-day and 12-month expectations. Every strategic engagement should end by grounding expectations here.

---

## 2026 median engagement rates by platform

Source: SociaVault Labs (350,000+ accounts, fraudulent accounts excluded), Q1 2026. See `research/external/2026-05-20-engagement-benchmarks-2026.md`.

| Platform | Median ER (2026) | YoY change | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 4.25% | -8.0% | Declining |
| YouTube | 3.06% | +3.4% | Growing |
| LinkedIn | 2.94% | +12.6% | Fastest-growing |
| Bluesky | 3-6% (Q1 2026) | New data | Strong (early-mover advantage) |
| Instagram | 1.81% | -6.7% | Declining |
| Threads | 1-3% | New data | Variable |
| X (Twitter) | 1.11% | -9.0% | Declining |

**Key takeaway for founders:** LinkedIn is the fastest-growing engagement platform with a stable, high-quality professional audience. X is declining. Bluesky has the highest engagement quality for its audience size.

> **Data limitation note:** SociaVault data is from Q3 2025 (covering Q1-Q2 2025 activity). The 2026 LinkedIn and Twitter/X numbers appear to be early 2026 extrapolations. Flag this when presenting to founders — treat as directional, not precise.

## Nano-tier advantage: why small accounts should optimize for ER, not follower count

At the nano tier (1K-10K followers), engagement rates are dramatically higher than at macro/mega tier.

| Tier | Followers | TikTok median ER | Instagram median ER |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K-10K | 7.84% | 3.42% |
| Micro | 10K-100K | 4.92% | 2.17% |
| Macro | 100K-1M | 2.31% | 1.06% |
| Mega | 1M+ | 1.84% | 0.81% |

The nano-to-mega ER drop is 4.3x (TikTok) to 4.2x (Instagram). The same dynamic applies across all platforms: smaller, more niche audiences engage more.

**What this means for founders:** A founder with 400 followers and a 7% engagement rate is building something more valuable than a founder with 40,000 followers and a 0.3% ER. The former has a responsive, interested audience. Optimizing for follower count before engagement quality is optimizing for the wrong metric.

## Realistic growth trajectories

These are based on consistent posting at minimum viable cadence (3x/week for LinkedIn/Bluesky, 5x/week for Threads, 3x/week + daily engagement for X) with authentic content.

### LinkedIn (starting from zero)

| Period | Followers | Expected ER | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 days | 50-150 | 4-7% | Mostly your existing network |
| 90 days | 150-500 | 3-6% | First non-network followers from good posts |
| 6 months | 400-1,500 | 2.5-5% | Consistent quality posts start compounding |
| 12 months | 1,000-5,000 | 2-4% | If one post goes semi-viral, higher end |

> **Algorithm note:** LinkedIn's algorithm "swings wildly" — a post can get 50,000 impressions one week and 2,000 the next with no apparent change (monolit.sh, 2026). Don't optimize for any single post's performance.

### Bluesky (starting from zero, 2026 early-mover window)

| Period | Followers | Expected ER | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 days | 30-100 | 5-9% | High ER from small engaged community |
| 90 days | 100-400 | 4-8% | Dev community discovers early active accounts |
| 6 months | 300-1,200 | 3-6% | Compounding from consistent presence |
| 12 months | 800-3,000 | 3-6% | Early-mover advantage persists while platform grows |

> **Bluesky window note:** Bluesky's early-mover advantage is real but narrowing as the platform grows. Founders who start building a Bluesky presence in 2026 will have an easier path than those who start in 2027.

### X (with Premium, starting from zero)

| Period | Followers | Expected ER | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 days | 20-80 | 2-4% | Slower start than LinkedIn |
| 90 days | 80-300 | 1.5-3% | Build-in-public community begins engaging |
| 6 months | 200-800 | 1-2% | If one thread goes viral, dramatic jump possible |
| 12 months | 500-2,000 | 1-1.5% | Premium helps, but X overall declining |

> **Without Premium:** Non-Premium accounts see 60% less reach than verified accounts with identical follower counts (Stanford Internet Observatory 2025). For new X accounts, Premium is effectively required for meaningful organic reach.

## Setting expectations in practice

When presenting a growth trajectory to a founder:

1. Use the realistic mid-range numbers, not the high end.
2. Emphasize engagement rate over follower count for the first 12 months.
3. Say explicitly: "Your 90-day goal is consistent posting and a rising engagement rate, not a follower count milestone."
4. If they ask "when will I hit 10,000 followers?" — answer with the ER question instead: "What matters more is whether the 500 followers you have are genuinely engaging. That's the signal that 10,000 will eventually follow."
5. Flag that algorithm instability means any single week's performance is noise; monthly or quarterly trends are signal.

---

## When to reassess the strategy

Flag a strategy reassessment if:
- 60 days of consistent posting with zero upward engagement trend
- ER drops below 1% on LinkedIn or 2% on Bluesky
- Follower growth is negative (net unfollows exceeding new follows)
- Founder is posting but has stopped engaging with comments

These signals usually indicate: wrong platform for the audience, voice that hasn't been found yet, or content type mismatch. Reassess using `guides/02-platform-selection.md` and `guides/03-founder-voice.md`.
