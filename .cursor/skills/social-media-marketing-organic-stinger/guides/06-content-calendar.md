# Guide 06: Content Calendar

The sustainable default: 3 posts/week. This guide produces a calendar the founder can maintain, not an aspirational one they will abandon.

---

## The honest bandwidth question

Before building any calendar, ask: "How many hours per week can you genuinely commit to social media content, including writing and engagement?"

Not the aspirational number. The number they would tell a friend who is skeptical. The number that survives a chaotic product week.

| Honest hours/week | Sustainable cadence |
|---|---|
| 1-2 hours | 2 posts/week, LinkedIn or Bluesky only |
| 2-4 hours | 3 posts/week (default) |
| 4-6 hours | 4-5 posts/week, can add secondary platform |
| 6+ hours | 5+ posts/week, secondary platform viable |

**The rule:** Scale up from the sustainable baseline only after 30 days of demonstrated consistency. A founder who says "I can do 5 posts/week" and produces 1 for 3 weeks is better served by starting at 2 and building.

---

## The three-post-per-week default

Three post types, one per slot:

| Slot | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Monday/Tuesday | **Insight post** | A principle, lesson, or observation from the founder's experience that is useful to anyone — whether they buy or not. Grounded in specific experience, not generic advice. |
| Wednesday/Thursday | **Build update post** | An honest process update. Could be a product decision, a milestone, a failure, a customer conversation. Numbers preferred. See `guides/04-build-in-public.md`. |
| Friday | **Engagement post** | A question, poll, or opinion the founder wants to discuss. Must be genuine — the founder needs to reply to every response. Can also be a reply or comment to a peer's popular post. |

These types map directly to the BIP 70/30 rule:
- Insight posts = the 70% valuable-to-anyone
- Build updates = the 30% product-specific
- Engagement posts = the distribution mechanism

---

## Platform-specific cadence adjustments

The above is the platform-agnostic default. Adjust per platform selection:

| Platform | Minimum viable cadence | Optimal cadence | Frequency of engagement replies |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | 2x/week | 3-5x/week | 15 min/day |
| Bluesky | 3x/week | 3-5x/week | 15-20 min/day |
| X | 3x/week + daily engagement | 5x/week + engagement | 20 min/day |
| Threads | 5x/week | 1-3x/day | 20-30 min/day |

**Important:** Threads' minimum viable cadence (5x/week) is higher than the 3-post default. If Threads is the primary platform, the calendar must be expanded or the founder must accept lower algorithmic reach.

## 4-week calendar scaffold (LinkedIn / Bluesky primary)

See `templates/content-calendar-4-week.md` for the fill-in template.

**Week 1: Baseline-setting**
- Mon: Insight — who you are, what you're building, what problem you're focused on
- Wed: Build update — where you are right now (numbers included)
- Fri: Engagement — question about a challenge your target customers face

**Week 2: Depth**
- Mon: Insight — one lesson from the last month of building
- Wed: Build update — a specific decision you made and why
- Fri: Engagement — a counterintuitive opinion about your space

**Week 3: Process**
- Mon: Insight — something you got wrong (failure + learning format)
- Wed: Build update — metrics or milestone (MRR, users, churn, whatever you have)
- Fri: Engagement — a genuine question where you want the audience's perspective

**Week 4: Community**
- Mon: Insight — a lesson from a customer conversation
- Wed: Build update — what you're shipping next and what decision led to that priority
- Fri: Engagement — a reply thread or perspective on something happening in your space

## The content lifespan factor

Platform content lifespan affects how strictly to batch and schedule:

- **LinkedIn:** 3-5 days. Posts can be written in advance and scheduled. Spacing of 1-2 days between posts is fine.
- **Bluesky:** Shorter lifespan (chronological feed). Daily posting is optimal; batch scheduling is still effective.
- **X:** Real-time platform. The first 30-minute velocity determines distribution. Schedule with precision; engage immediately after posting.
- **Threads:** 6-12 hours. Needs higher frequency and daily fresh content; scheduling less effective than real-time posting.

---

## The "content debt" trap

Founders often binge-post to make up for missed weeks. This is counterproductive:
1. Binge-posting doesn't compensate algorithmically — consistent 3x/week beats 15 posts in one week
2. Binge-posting burns the founder out, making the next gap worse
3. Audiences notice the pattern and disengage

**The remedy:** When behind, restart the 3x/week cadence without making up for the gap. Never try to "catch up."

---

## Adding a second platform

Add a secondary platform only when:
1. 60+ consecutive days of primary platform cadence maintained
2. Primary platform engagement rate is trending up (not just holding)
3. Founder has 30+ minutes/week of extra capacity they are actually using for other things

When adding a second platform: repurpose (rewrite natively), do not cross-post. The primary platform cadence must not drop. If it does, pause the secondary platform and return to primary focus.
