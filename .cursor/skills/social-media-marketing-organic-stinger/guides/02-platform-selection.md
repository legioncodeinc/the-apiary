# Guide 02: Platform Selection

The platform-fit rubric for solo developers and founders. The output is always: **one primary platform** with a clear rationale, and optionally one secondary.

---

## The four platforms in scope (2026 state)

### LinkedIn
- **Audience:** 1.1B members, B2B professionals, decision-makers, investors, operators
- **Algorithm:** Slow-pace (3-5 day content lifespan), personal profiles dramatically outperform company pages, rewards long-form and carousel formats
- **Engagement rate (2026):** 2.94% median, fastest-growing platform (+12.6% YoY)
- **Posting cadence:** 3-5x/week sustainable
- **Strengths:** Highest qualified-lead potential for B2B, professional credibility, newsletter integration
- **Weaknesses:** Algorithm instability ("swings wildly," monolit.sh), no paid boost needed but organic reach can be unpredictable
- **Critical rule:** Post from personal profile, NOT company page. LinkedIn personal profiles get dramatically higher organic reach in 2026.

### X (Twitter)
- **Audience:** 400M+ active users, general B2B, news, thought leadership, indie hacker community
- **Algorithm:** For You (algorithmic), prioritizes replies > likes, first-30-minute velocity critical, Premium verification provides 2-4x distribution boost
- **Engagement rate (2026):** 1.11% median, declining -9.0% YoY
- **Posting cadence:** 3-5x/week text-only; daily engagement replies
- **Strengths:** Largest B2B audience, build-in-public community historically strongest here
- **Weaknesses:** Premium verification effectively required for meaningful reach ($8-16/month), external links suppressed 30-50%, declining overall engagement
- **Critical rule:** External links reduce reach 30-50%. Put URLs in the first reply, not the post body.

### Bluesky
- **Audience:** 40M+ active users (2026), heavily developer/founder/tech-adjacent, early-adopter demographic
- **Algorithm:** Chronological default, optional custom feeds, no opaque algorithmic ranking, no ads as of 2026
- **Engagement rate (2026):** 3-6% median (Q1 2026 data), 4.2% in earlier 2025 measurements
- **Posting cadence:** 3-5x/week
- **Strengths:** Highest ER of text-focused platforms, early-mover advantage (smaller, less noise), level playing field (no paid promotion), dev/tech community concentration
- **Weaknesses:** 1/66th the reach of Twitter/X, smaller audience absolute size
- **Key insight for 2026:** For developers and SaaS founders targeting technical buyers or dev-adjacent professionals — Bluesky is the default recommendation. Early-mover advantage is real and diminishing.

### Threads
- **Audience:** 300M MAU (Athenic 2025), consumer, lifestyle, general — strong Instagram crossover
- **Algorithm:** Algorithmic (Meta-derived), rewards visual content, discovery-oriented
- **Engagement rate (2026):** 1-3% median
- **Posting cadence:** 5-7x/week minimum for meaningful reach
- **Strengths:** Best for discovery (can reach non-followers), consumer B2C brands, conversational tone
- **Weaknesses:** Short content lifespan (6-12 hours vs LinkedIn's 3-5 days), 5-7x/week cadence is unsustainable for most solo founders, algorithmic unpredictability, low lead-gen potential for B2B

---

## The selection rubric

Apply these questions in order. The first decisive answer determines the recommendation.

### 1. Who is your target customer?
- Technical founders, developers, dev-adjacent professionals → **Bluesky primary**
- Business operators, executives, investors, B2B buyers → **LinkedIn primary**
- General consumers, lifestyle buyers, community builders → **Threads primary**
- Journalists, news-readers, general thought leadership → **X primary** (budget for Premium)

### 2. What is your realistic weekly posting commitment?
- 3-5 posts/week → LinkedIn or Bluesky are viable
- 1-3 posts/day → Threads becomes viable (still demanding)
- Under 3 posts/week → LinkedIn only (3-day content lifespan makes infrequent posting less punishing)

### 3. Do you have an existing Instagram audience?
- Yes → Threads gains a headstart from Instagram cross-follow; consider as secondary
- No → Threads starting from zero has a high cadence requirement that may not justify the investment

### 4. Is budget a constraint?
- Tight budget → Bluesky (no pay-to-play, level field), LinkedIn (organic first, no Premium required)
- Willing to pay $8-16/month → X Premium opens meaningful reach; without it, X is difficult for new accounts

---

## Recommended starting configurations

| Founder type | Primary | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Solo dev / open-source / dev tools | Bluesky | LinkedIn (add at 90 days) |
| B2B SaaS (non-technical buyer) | LinkedIn | Bluesky or X with Premium |
| Consumer product / lifestyle | Threads | LinkedIn if also B2B |
| Indie hacker with existing X presence | X (with Premium) | Bluesky |
| Zero social presence starting fresh | LinkedIn | none until 90 days consistent |

---

## What "one platform for 90 days" actually means

The 90-day rule is not arbitrary. It takes approximately 90 days of consistent posting at minimum cadence for platform algorithms to recognize an account as a reliable signal and begin distributing to non-followers. Accounts that jump between platforms restart this clock on each one.

During the 90 days: post at cadence, engage daily, produce at least one piece of genuinely valuable content per week. At the end: assess engagement rate vs. the benchmarks in `guides/07-growth-benchmarks.md`. If on track, add the secondary platform.
