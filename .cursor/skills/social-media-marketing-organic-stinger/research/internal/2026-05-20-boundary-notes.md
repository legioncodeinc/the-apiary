# Boundary Notes: Peer Bee Relationships

## Summary

`social-media-marketing-organic-worker-bee` has explicit peer relationships with two Bees. These boundaries define the scope ceiling and handoff triggers for the stinger's guidance.

## Boundary 1: newsletter-platform-worker-bee

### Where social-media-marketing-organic-worker-bee ends

The Bee owns social-first growth including content designed to build email list awareness. The moment a strategy decision centers on "converting social followers to email subscribers" or "choosing a newsletter platform," the work belongs to `newsletter-platform-worker-bee`.

### Handoff trigger

"A social post is designed to drive email signups" — at that inflection point, `newsletter-platform-worker-bee` takes the conversion strategy.

### What this means for the stinger

`guides/08-engagement-strategy.md` can reference that social content may feed email signups, but must not prescribe which newsletter platform to use, how to set up welcome sequences, or how to grow the email list itself.

### newsletter-platform-worker-bee scope (for context)

- Platform selection (Beehiiv, Kit, Loops, Substack, Resend)
- Embedded signup integration
- Deliverability tradeoffs
- Monetization options

## Boundary 2: seo-aeo-worker-bee

### Where social-media-marketing-organic-worker-bee ends

This Bee owns social-platform-native content strategy. `seo-aeo-worker-bee` owns search-driven content strategy. The two are peers, not subordinates.

### Key distinction

- Social-native content: Written for platform algorithms, social graph distribution, and community engagement. Optimized for scroll-stop, not search intent.
- Search-native content: Written to rank in Google / Bing / AI Overviews. Optimized for crawlability, keyword targeting, and E-E-A-T signals.

### Where they might appear to overlap

A founder's LinkedIn article might be indexed by Google. That does NOT make it SEO content — it is platform-native content that happens to be indexed. The authoring decisions (voice, structure, format, call to action) belong to this Bee; any optimization for search ranking belongs to seo-aeo-worker-bee.

### What this means for the stinger

The stinger must not prescribe keyword research, meta descriptions, structured data, or Core Web Vitals. It may reference that authentic social content can improve brand search visibility as a secondary effect, but search strategy is explicitly out of scope.

- Do not include newsletter platform recommendations anywhere in the stinger.
- Do not include SEO advice, keyword strategy, or search ranking tactics.
- The handoff language ("once a social post is designed to drive email signups, route to newsletter-platform-worker-bee") should appear verbatim or close to verbatim in `guides/08-engagement-strategy.md`.
