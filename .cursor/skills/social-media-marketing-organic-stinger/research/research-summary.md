# Research Summary: social-media-marketing-organic-stinger

## Five most influential sources

1. **`external/2026-05-20-engagement-benchmarks-2026.md`** (SociaVault Labs, Sep 2025) — 350K+ account analysis, per-platform ER tables, nano-vs-mega tier data, fraud prevalence (37.2%). The quantitative backbone for growth benchmarks and the anti-bought-follower argument.

2. **`external/2026-05-20-ai-content-detection-linkedin.md`** (Foundera, May 2026) — LinkedIn 360Brew model details: 20-40% reach penalty for pure AI posts, 0-100 authenticity score, hybrid post equivalence. The primary evidence for the AI anti-pattern cost.

3. **`external/2026-05-20-anti-ai-backlash-algorithms.md`** (Storrito, Mar 2026) — Instagram and LinkedIn algorithm shifts rewarding comment depth, DM shares, and original content. 30% consumer backlash stat against AI ads.

4. **`external/2026-05-20-platform-algorithm-comparison.md`** (Athenic, May 2025) — Engagement rates per platform (Bluesky 4.2%, LinkedIn 2.94%, Threads 2.1%, X 1.5%), X Premium requirement analysis, "60% focus on one primary platform" finding.

5. **`external/2026-05-20-build-in-public-x-playbook.md`** (Founder Distro, Mar 2026) — 70/30 content split, failure-posts-outperform-success-posts 3-5x finding, X algorithm 2026 priorities (replies > likes, first-30-min velocity, Premium boost).

## Key findings by guide area

### guides/00-principles.md
- 37.2% of social accounts show fraudulent activity (SociaVault) — bought followers destroy meaningful benchmarks
- 30% of consumers less likely to choose a brand using AI-generated ads (Storrito)
- LinkedIn 360Brew penalizes lazy AI use (20-40% reach loss) but not thoughtful hybrid use (Foundera)
- "Your unfakeable human experience is the differentiator" (For The Love of Socials)

### guides/01-anti-pattern-catalog.md
- X Premium required for 2-4x distribution — non-verified accounts at 60% disadvantage (Athenic)
- AI-generated content that attracts AI-generated comments creates self-reinforcing low-quality loop (Storrito)
- LinkedIn: >2 em-dashes + 0 ellipses flags post as AI-generated (Foundera)
- Instagram now weights engagement depth over volume; 50 substantive comments beats 500 passive likes (Storrito)

### guides/02-platform-selection.md
- Platform matrix: LinkedIn (1.1B, 3-5 days content lifespan, B2B leads), X (400M+, real-time, Premium required), Threads (300M, 6-12 hours, conversational), Bluesky (40M, 4.2% ER, no ads, dev/tech community)
- Solo developers: Bluesky first (tech audience, level playing field, 3-5x/week sustainable)
- LinkedIn: personal profiles dramatically outperform company pages in 2026

### guides/03-founder-voice.md
- "Document your process, not just your highlights" (For The Love of Socials)
- "AI can write about your product but can't write about the specific customer conversation you had on a Tuesday"
- Voice mining from Slack/email/casual conversation, not imposed brand voice templates

### guides/04-build-in-public.md
- 70/30 rule: 70% useful insights for anyone, 30% product updates (Founder Distro)
- Failure posts outperform success posts 3-5x (Founder Distro)
- Numbers are not optional — revenue, users, churn, MRR (Founder Distro)
- 70-20-10 Rule for X: 70% value, 20% engagement, 10% promotional (Monolit/indie-hacker)

### guides/07-growth-benchmarks.md
- LinkedIn fastest-growing ER platform: 2.22% (2024) → 2.94% (2026), +12.6% YoY
- Twitter/X declining: -9.0% YoY, now 1.11% median ER
- Nano-tier (1K-10K) TikTok: 7.84% median ER — nano outperforms mega 4.3x
- Bluesky 3-6% ER, Threads 1-3% ER (Q1 2026)

## Open questions surviving research

1. **X/Threads AI detection specifics** — LinkedIn has quantified penalties (20-40%); X and Threads detection mechanisms and penalties are not yet quantified. Flag in guides as "mechanism unknown, best practice unchanged."

2. **Bluesky 2026 algorithm** — Bluesky added custom feeds but remains chronologically-defaulted. Whether a Bluesky-specific algorithm is in development was not surfaced. Flag as "no opaque algorithm yet" in guides/02.

3. **Founder-led content vs brand account quantitative data** — The qualitative evidence is strong (consistent narrative), but the specific quantitative lift for B2B SaaS founder-vs-brand was not found in a controlled study. Use qualitative evidence and the general "personal profiles outperform company pages on LinkedIn" finding.

4. **2026 cross-posting penalties** — Research surfaces the engagement quality degradation from cross-posting but does not show a specific algorithmic penalty. The argument is primarily audience experience + format mismatch, not confirmed algorithmic suppression.
