# 02 — Writing Objectives

How to author aspirational, well-formed Objectives that pass the Grove/Doerr quality bar.

*Sources: `research/external/2026-05-20-grove-doerr-okr-canon.md`, `research/external/2026-05-20-okr-writing-objectives.md`, `research/external/2026-05-20-google-rework-okr-guide.md`*

---

## The Objective rubric

A well-formed Objective passes all five tests:

| Test | Pass criteria |
|---|---|
| **Aspirational** | Makes you slightly uncomfortable to commit to |
| **Qualitative** | Describes a direction, not a number |
| **Memorable** | Fits on a sticky note; your team can recall it verbatim |
| **Time-bound** | Implicitly or explicitly scoped to the cycle |
| **Outcome, not project** | Describes a state of the world, not work items |

The Google re:Work guide adds one more: *"If the Objective doesn't make you at least a little nervous, it's probably not ambitious enough."*

---

## The "best customer" test

For product Objectives, ask: *"Would our best, most engaged customers be proud of this outcome?"* If the answer is "probably not" or "they wouldn't notice," the Objective is too operational.

Compare:
- "Reduce P1 bug backlog by 50%" → operational KPI, not an Objective
- "Build a product our best customers tell their colleagues about" → aspirational Objective

The metric reduction belongs in a Key Result underneath the second version.

## The four Objective failure modes

### 1. The KPI target disguised as an Objective
> "Reach $2M ARR in Q3"

This is a Key Result (measurable outcome), not an Objective. Objectives are qualitative. Fix:
> "Build a go-to-market motion that makes $2M ARR feel inevitable" (Objective)  
> Key Result: "ARR grows from $1.1M to $2M by Sept 30" (KR)

### 2. The project milestone
> "Launch the new onboarding flow"

Launching is work. The Objective should capture why it matters. Fix:
> "Make new users successful in their first 10 minutes"  
> Key Result: "Time-to-first-value drops from 4 days to 45 minutes"

### 3. The department function statement
> "Run excellent product operations"

This describes a job description, not a direction. Fix:
> "Eliminate the product-to-engineering handoff drag that slows every cycle"  
> Key Result: "Spec-to-sprint-start time drops from 3 weeks to 5 days"

### 4. The too-safe hedge
> "Continue to improve customer satisfaction"

"Continue to improve" signals sandbagging. Objectives should describe a step change, not maintenance. Fix:
> "Become the obvious choice for customers who care about quality"

## Writing Objectives step by step

**Step 1: Name the domain.** What is the most important thing to change this quarter? (Product, growth, infrastructure, team, market position?)

**Step 2: Write the desired state.** If this quarter goes perfectly, what is true that isn't true today? Write it as a sentence, not a headline.

**Step 3: Apply the five tests.** Is it aspirational? Qualitative? Memorable? Time-bound? Outcome-oriented? Tighten until all five pass.

**Step 4: Gut-check the ambition level.** Apply the "slightly uncomfortable" test. If you're 100% confident you'll achieve this, it's sandbagged. If you're confident you can't, it's a fantasy. Target the zone where the team believes it's possible but not guaranteed.

**Step 5: Confirm it's not a Key Result.** If you can assign a number to it, it's a KR, not an Objective. Move the number to a KR and rewrite the Objective as the "why" behind that number.

## Rewrite patterns

| Weak Objective | Fix | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| "Improve reliability" | "Make reliability a competitive advantage, not an apology" | Qualitative, aspirational, memorable |
| "Hit Q3 revenue target" | "Prove our sales motion works at scale" | KR becomes a KR; Objective captures the strategic intent |
| "Reduce technical debt" | "Eliminate the engineering drag that's slowing every feature cycle" | Connects the work to business impact |
| "Launch mobile app" | "Open a new growth channel through mobile" | Milestone becomes Objective with purpose |
| "Q2 Engineering OKR" | "Build the platform our next 10 engineers can ship safely from day one" | Name tells the story |
