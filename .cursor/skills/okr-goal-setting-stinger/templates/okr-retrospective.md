## Part 2: Retrospective questions (30-60 minutes)

Each participant answers questions 1-3 individually before the group discussion. Questions 4-7 are discussed as a group.

### Individual reflection (5-10 minutes, written)

**Q1:** Which OKR result surprised you most — a score higher or lower than you expected? Why?

> *(Space for individual answers)*

**Q2:** Which Key Result was the most honest stretch target? Which was the most sandbagged? What made the difference?

> *(Space for individual answers)*

**Q3:** Did your team's actual work this quarter reflect the OKRs, or were the real priorities elsewhere? Be specific.

> *(Space for individual answers)*

---

### Group discussion (30-45 minutes)

**Q4:** What did this OKR cycle teach us that we did not know at the start? (Learning extraction)

> *(Group notes)*

**Q5:** Which blockers appeared that our OKRs did not capture? Should they have? (OKR coverage check)

> *(Group notes)*

**Q6:** What would we change about the OKR process next cycle — cadence, format, tooling, ownership, or number of OKRs? (Process improvement)

> *(Group notes)*

**Q7:** What baseline data do we need to establish in Week 1 of the next cycle to avoid repeating the "we don't have a baseline" problem? (Next-cycle readiness)

> *(Group notes)*
