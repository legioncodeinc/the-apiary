## Answers to Brief's Open Questions

### Q1: Is Termly's 2026 generator current with CCPA/CPRA amendments and Quebec Law 25?

**CCPA/CPRA:** Yes. Confirmed current as of January 2026. Termly explicitly covers CPRA, CalOPPA, Virginia CDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA.

**Quebec Law 25:** Not confirmed. Neither Termly nor Iubenda explicitly list Law 25 compliance. The generator-landscape-2026 source notes that both generators "often catch up months after a regulation actually starts being enforced." **Recommendation for stinger:** Flag that generator output alone is insufficient for Law 25; attorney review with Law 25 checklist is required.

### Q2: Status of EU-US Data Privacy Framework for DPA cross-border clauses?

**Status:** DPF is valid and operational (adopted July 2023, first periodic review 2024 confirmed functioning). For DPA design:
- DPF-certified US processors: DPF adequacy reference in DPA, no SCCs needed
- Non-DPF-certified US processors: 2021 SCCs Module 2 required
- GDPR Art. 28 DPA always required regardless of transfer mechanism
- **Risk flag:** DPF has been subject to ongoing legal challenges (Schrems III risk); stinger should include DPF monitoring as a refresh trigger

### Q3: Has LGPD enforcement matured enough to require a materially different DPA?

**Research finding:** The research did not surface 2025-2026 LGPD-specific practitioner guidance strong enough to answer this definitively. Available sources (generator-landscape-2026) confirm LGPD is in the "seven framework" coverage list but do not provide Brazil-specific DPA differentiation guidance. **Recommendation for stinger-forge:** Note LGPD in the compliance matrix as "GDPR-analogous DPA structure; ANPD enforcement maturing; consult Brazil-specialized attorney for material Brazil exposure." Flag as an open question for follow-up research.
