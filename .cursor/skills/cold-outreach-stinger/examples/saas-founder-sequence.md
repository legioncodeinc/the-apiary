# Example: SaaS Founder Cold Email Sequence

**Scenario:** A founder selling a sales coaching platform (helps reps improve discovery calls through AI-powered call analysis) targeting VP of Sales at B2B SaaS companies with 50-500 employees.

**Guides used:** `guides/03-sequence-design.md`, `guides/01-tool-decision-matrix.md`
**Template used:** `templates/sequence-5-step.md`

---

## ICP and setup

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| ICP | VP of Sales at B2B SaaS, 50-500 employees, US |
| Tool stack | Apollo (list) + Instantly (send) |
| Sending domain | trycoachiq.com |
| Sequence length | 4 steps (SMB, email-only) |
| Target reply rate | >3% (industry average) |

## Buying trigger applied

VP of Sales who posted on LinkedIn about missing quota in Q1, OR company has open SDR/AE job postings (signals active sales investment).

---

## Sequence

### Step 1 (Day 0) — Initial outreach

**Subject:** Discovery call win rate at {{Company}}

**Body:**

```
[Claygent opener goes here — e.g., "Your thread on why the first 90-second question is the most important thing in discovery resonated with how we built CoachIQ."]

Most VP Sales I talk to say reps can follow a great discovery framework but still miss the buying signal in the first two minutes. We built an AI layer that flags the exact moment a prospect signals intent — and trains reps to respond before the window closes.

15 minutes Thursday or Friday?

Alex
```

**Notes:**
- Opener is genuine, specific (references a real post or signal from Claygent)
- Problem is precise: "miss the buying signal in the first two minutes" — not "improve sales performance"
- CTA: specific time options, not "would love to connect"

---

### Step 2 (Day 3) — Follow-up

**Subject:** Re: Discovery call win rate at {{Company}}

**Body:**

```
Sent this over a couple days ago — sharing one thing that might be useful regardless:

[If hiring signal]: Noticed {{Company}} is hiring [role]. When we helped teams onboarding new reps, the biggest unlock was getting consistent discovery behavior in the first 30 days — rather than fixing bad habits 6 months in.

Worth a quick chat?

Alex
```

**Notes:**
- Reads like a reply, not a reminder
- References new context (hiring signal if available, otherwise a fresh insight)
- Still one CTA

---

### Step 3 (Day 8) — Case study angle

**Subject:** How [Similar Company] went from 23% to 41% win rate in 90 days

**Body:**

```
{{FirstName}},

Sharing a quick case study in case it's more tangible than my usual pitch:

[Similar company, same ICP] was struggling with reps who could run discovery by the book but still couldn't convert. We found the gap was in listening, not asking — reps were missing the nuances after the prospect answered.

In 90 days, win rate went from 23% to 41%. Happy to share the methodology if useful.

Still worth 15 minutes?

Alex
```

**Notes:**
- Different angle from steps 1-2 (data/outcome vs problem frame)
- Specific number (23% → 41%), not "significantly improved"
- Soft close — "if useful" reduces pressure

---

### Step 4 (Day 18) — Breakup

**Subject:** Closing the loop

**Body:**

```
{{FirstName}},

I won't keep reaching out after this one.

If improving discovery win rate ever becomes a priority, I'm easy to find. If not — no hard feelings at all.

Alex
```

**Notes:**
- Short, genuine, no pressure
- Does not include a case study or pitch — this is about removing pressure
- Often generates a "sorry, crazy quarter — actually let's talk" reply
