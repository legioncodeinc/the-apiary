# 06 - CTA Rubric

A CTA (call-to-action) is a specific offer to a reader who has just consumed content. It is not a request for a favor, a plea for a like, or a generic invitation to "learn more." Every CTA must pass the "why now" test before it goes on the page.

> Research sources: `research/external/2026-05-20-unbounce-cta-examples-2025.md`, `research/external/2026-05-20-automateed-blog-cta-ideas-strategies.md`

---

## The "why now" test

Before writing any CTA, answer these three questions:

1. **What specifically happens after clicking?** (Not "you'll learn more" — name the specific deliverable, resource, or experience.)
2. **Why is this worth doing right now?** (What makes this moment — after reading this post — the right time to act?)
3. **Does this imply the reader owes anything to the author?** (If yes, rewrite. The reader owes nothing. The CTA must be an offer, not a request.)

A CTA that fails any of these three questions does not go live.

---

## Anti-beg protocols

These phrases and patterns are banned:

| Banned pattern | Why | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| "Please subscribe" | Implies the reader owes a favor | "Get [specific newsletter/update] every [cadence]" |
| "If you enjoyed this..." | Conditional flattery; signals low confidence | Remove entirely; let the CTA stand alone |
| "Don't forget to share" | Solicitation framed as a reminder | Remove; if the post is good, sharing happens |
| "Learn more" | Vague; no outcome | "[Specific outcome verb]: compare all 7 tools side-by-side" |
| "Click here" | No context; no outcome | "Download the [specific] template" |
| False urgency | "Only 3 spots left" when there are always spots | Remove urgency unless real |
| Generic "Sign up free" | Too transactional for an informational post | "Start your 7-day trial and test [specific feature]" |

## CTA placement models

| Placement | Best for | Rules |
|---|---|---|
| **Above fold (hero area)** | High-intent landing pages; not typical for most blog posts | One sentence context + button; do not compete with the post content |
| **Mid-post inline** | Posts with a natural "pause point" after a strong section or checklist | Context-matched: CTA should relate directly to the section it follows |
| **End-of-post** | All blog posts as the default | One-line benefit recap + button; this is where the reader's intent is highest |
| **Sticky bar** | High-traffic posts where conversion is the primary goal | Only if it does not obscure content on mobile; use sparingly |

**Rule: One primary CTA per post.** Multiple competing CTAs create decision paralysis and reduce conversion. Choose one action that serves the reader's primary next step.

## CTA copy formulas by funnel stage

**Awareness stage (informational posts)**
The reader just learned something. The CTA should offer more of the same value, not push for a purchase.
- Formula: "Get [topic] [format] [frequency/scope]"
- Example: "Get the weekly content strategy digest — 3 actionable frameworks per issue, no filler."

**Consideration stage (commercial-investigation posts)**
The reader is evaluating options. The CTA should reduce friction in the decision.
- Formula: "Try [product] free [duration/scope] — no [barrier they fear]"
- Example: "Start your Semrush 7-day trial — no credit card required."

**Decision stage (transactional intent posts)**
The reader is ready to act. The CTA should name the outcome and remove doubt.
- Formula: "[Outcome verb] [specific thing] [optional urgency if real]"
- Example: "Download the cluster-map template — 12 pillar + cluster examples pre-filled."

---

## CTA for lead magnets

Lead-magnet CTAs are the highest-converting CTA type for informational blogs. The formula:

```
[Outcome verb] the [specific deliverable] — [one-line description of what's inside]
```

Examples:
- "Download the pre-publish checklist — all 12 points as a printable PDF."
- "Get the cluster-map template — 3 filled-in examples for B2B SaaS, agency, and developer tools."
- "Grab the keyword scoping worksheet — 4-question filter for any cluster hypothesis."

**Rule for lead-magnet CTAs:** Name the deliverable specifically. "Get the free guide" is weaker than "Get the 12-step content audit guide (PDF, 14 pages)."

## Testing sequence

If CTAs are underperforming, test in this order:

1. **Copy first.** Change button text and the surrounding sentence. A/B test for 30 days.
2. **Placement second.** Move the CTA from end-of-post to mid-post (after the strongest section) and measure lift.
3. **Design third.** Only after copy and placement are validated, test color, size, or button style.

Most CTA underperformance is a copy problem. Do not jump to design changes before validating copy.

---

## Quick-check: three questions before publishing

1. Does the CTA answer "what specifically happens after clicking"?
2. Is there exactly one CTA per post?
3. Does the copy contain any of the banned patterns above?

If any answer is "no" or "yes" (for the third), revise before the post goes live.
