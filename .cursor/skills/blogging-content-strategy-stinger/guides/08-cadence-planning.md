# 08 - Cadence Planning

A publishing cadence is only useful if it is sustainable. This guide gives you the capacity model to set a cadence the team can maintain for six months, and the structural rules that make that cadence effective.

> Research sources: `research/external/2026-05-20-averi-content-planning-startups-2026.md`, `research/external/2026-05-20-incremys-content-calendar-method-2026.md`, `research/external/2026-05-20-averi-content-calendar-that-works.md`

---

## The capacity model

Express cadence in time investment (hours/week), not just posts/month.

| Team situation | Hours/week available for content | Sustainable posts/month | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solo founder, no AI writing tools | 2-3 hrs/week | 1-2 posts | One cluster article + one support article; no pillar per month |
| Solo founder, AI-assisted | 5 hrs/week | 2-4 posts | Achievable with AI drafting + human editing; one monthly pillar |
| 2-person team (one content-focused) | 8-10 hrs/week | 4-6 posts | One pillar + 3-5 cluster articles |
| Small team with dedicated writer | 15+ hrs/week | 6-10 posts | Sustainable with editorial calendar + production workflow |

**Rule of thumb:** Each post (cluster article, 1,200-1,800 words) takes 3-4 hours from brief to publish without AI assistance; 1-2 hours with AI assistance for drafting.

---

## The non-negotiable structural rules

These rules apply regardless of cadence level:

1. **Strategy map before calendar.** Set the cluster map (see `guides/01-cluster-pillar-architecture.md`) before scheduling any posts. A calendar without a cluster map produces isolated posts.
2. **Pillar before cluster content.** Never schedule a cluster article before its parent pillar is published or firmly scheduled in the same month.
3. **4-6 week production runway.** The calendar slot is the publish date. Work backwards: brief at D-14, draft at D-7, SEO review at D-3, final edit + meta at D-1, publish on D.
4. **70/30 evergreen/timely ratio.** Target 70% evergreen cluster content (long shelf life, builds authority) and 30% timely or reactive pieces (news, product updates, trending topics). Inverting this ratio produces traffic that is event-driven and does not compound.
5. **Maintain 2-4 buffer posts.** Always have 2-4 fully-written, ready-to-publish evergreen posts in reserve. This is the cadence resilience reserve — for weeks when a post falls through or the team gets sick.
6. **Monthly refresh cycle.** Set a monthly calendar event to: (a) identify posts with GSC traffic drops > 20% MoM, (b) update outdated statistics or product names, (c) add new PAA questions surfaced since publication, (d) check internal links for dead pages.

## The production workflow

```
D-14: Brief (using templates/post-brief-template.md)
D-7:  Draft complete (handed off for review or self-reviewed after 24-hour gap)
D-3:  SEO review (keyword check, meta complete, internal links confirmed)
D-1:  Final edit + pre-publish checklist (guides/07-pre-publish-checklist.md)
D:    Publish + internal link update on parent pillar or cluster articles
D+7:  GSC indexing confirmed; submit URL via Search Console if not indexed
```

**For solo founders:** The 24-hour self-review gap between draft completion and final edit is not optional. Writing and reviewing in the same session produces blind spots. Rest, then read as a reader — not as the author.

## Realistic domain authority ramp

These are the realistic organic growth timelines from the research (see `research/external/2026-05-20-seo-content-clusters-topical-authority.md`):

| Phase | Timeline | What to expect |
|---|---|---|
| Indexing + base establishment | 0-3 months | Posts indexed; minimal traffic; no authority signals yet |
| First cluster consolidation | 3-6 months | Long-tail cluster articles begin ranking; pillar gets first impressions |
| Visible topical halo | 9-12 months | New posts in established clusters rank faster than in month 1; referral traffic begins |
| Domain-level authority | 12-24 months | Cross-cluster authority; pillar pages competing for mid-difficulty keywords |

**Do not evaluate the content strategy before the 6-month mark.** Google's crawl and consolidation cycles mean that content published in month 1 often does not show meaningful rankings until month 3-4.

## Setting expectations with stakeholders

When presenting a cadence plan to a founder, investor, or team, use this framing:

> "We will publish [N] posts per month, focused on [cluster topic]. By month [3-6], we expect initial cluster rankings for long-tail terms. By month [9-12], we expect the first visible topical authority signal. Traffic compound-growth begins at month [12]. This is the realistic timeline for organic content; we do not expect significant organic traffic before month 6."

If the stakeholder needs traffic before month 6, organic content is not the primary channel for that timeline — paid acquisition or social distribution may be more appropriate, and should be discussed separately.

## Cadence for updating existing posts

New posts are not always the highest-ROI content action. Refreshing high-impression/low-CTR existing posts (identified via GSC) often produces faster ranking improvement than publishing net-new.

**Monthly refresh priority order:**
1. Posts with >500 impressions but <2% CTR → fix title and meta first.
2. Posts that ranked well but dropped >30% in 90 days → update statistics, add new PAA sections.
3. Posts with thin internal links (< 2 links per 1,000 words) → add cluster article links.
4. New-post creation → after the refresh queue is empty.

**Rule:** A calendar that schedules only new posts and never refreshes will plateau. Allocate at least 20% of content time to refreshes after the first 6 months.
