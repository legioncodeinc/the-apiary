## Open Questions for stinger-forge

### Q1: Christensen Interview vs Moesta Switch Interview - which is the default?

The research surfaces two distinct JTBD interview methodologies: Christensen's exploratory interview (discovers *what the job is*) and Moesta's switch interview (reconstructs *how a specific purchase decision happened*). The Command Brief does not specify which to use as the default. The practitioner consensus is "Christensen first to find the job, Moesta second to understand purchase dynamics." `stinger-forge` should define the recommended sequencing in `guides/04-jtbd-interview.md` and decide whether to encode both or just Moesta (which Torres' practice most closely resembles).

### Q2: `library/discovery/` folder - define in stinger or defer to `library-worker-bee`?

The Command Brief explicitly flags this as an open question. The research shows Torres' methodology maps cleanly to a fixed folder structure. Recommendation: The stinger should define the `library/discovery/` schema with a note that `library-worker-bee` governs `library/` overall but `discovery/` is the discovery Bee's namespace. `stinger-forge` should draft the folder spec in `guides/00-principles.md` and surface it for the user to confirm.

### Q3: How does the Bee handle teams with no existing interview practice?

Torres' cadence assumes the trio is already doing some research. The research notes that fewer than 1 in 5 teams hit the weekly benchmark. The stinger needs a "Day 1" playbook for teams starting from zero: how to write the first research outline, how to set up an in-app recruiting trigger, how to structure the first 3 interviews without an existing OST. This should probably be `guides/03-interview-cadence.md`'s "Bootstrap" section. `stinger-forge` should decide whether to include a worked example or reference the `examples/` folder.

### Q4: AI-powered interview moderation - endorse, warn, or ignore?

Torres endorses AI-moderated async interviewing (via Vistaly partnership) as a legitimate way to lower the recruiting/scheduling tax. The getperspective.ai article goes further and recommends it as the operational default for 2026. The stinger should take a position: (a) endorse AI moderation as an acceleration tool while requiring human trio review of synthesis, (b) warn that AI moderation trades depth for scale (async interviews miss non-verbal cues, follow-up quality depends on AI), or (c) present both options and let the team decide. Torres' own framing ("Do both. Develop your discovery skills. Let AI do some.") suggests option (a).

### Q5: When does the Bee trigger vs. when does it hand off?

The Command Brief defines trigger conditions and handoff points abstractly. The stinger needs concrete trigger phrases (e.g., "we don't know what to build next", "we need to understand our users", "is this the right opportunity to pursue") and concrete handoff artifacts (desired-outcome.md exists and is agreed, OST has a target opportunity, winning solution has at least one validated assumption). `stinger-forge` should codify these as the Bee's entry/exit conditions in `guides/00-principles.md`.
