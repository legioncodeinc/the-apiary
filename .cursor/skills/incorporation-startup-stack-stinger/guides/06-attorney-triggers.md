# Guide 06: Attorney Triggers

Step 7: Know when to stop the DIY flow and engage a real attorney.

*This stinger produces informed guidance, not legal advice. These triggers are the explicit "get counsel" thresholds.*

---

## When a human attorney is required

### 1. International holding structure

If the founder or any entity in the ownership chain is:
- A foreign corporation or trust
- Using a Cayman Islands holdco → Delaware LLC → Delaware C-Corp structure for tax efficiency
- Structuring equity for investors in multiple countries

**Why:** International holding structures involve treaty-based tax optimization, Form 5471/5472 IRS reporting requirements, and cross-border IP transfer rules that have material tax consequences. Formation platforms cannot navigate this.

**Action:** Refer founder to a US tax attorney with international startup experience (YC Legal Resource list, Orrick, Goodwin Procter, Wilson Sonsini).

---

### 2. Complex IP ownership before formation

If any founder:
- Was employed at another company and wrote code or created IP that could arguably belong to that employer
- Has open-source contributions that might conflict with the company's licensing model
- Participated in a university research program where IP ownership may be shared with the institution
- Has prior inventions they want to specifically exclude from the IP assignment

**Why:** IP ownership disputes post-formation are expensive. An attorney can draft a clean IP carve-out or get a written release from the prior employer/institution before signing the PIIA.

**Action:** Do not sign the IP assignment until an attorney reviews the specific prior-IP situation.

---

### 3. Co-founder equity dispute or pre-formation tension

If any of the following apply:
- Co-founders disagree on equity split and have not yet resolved it in writing
- One co-founder is joining later than the others and the "founding equity" conversation is unresolved
- Any co-founder has an existing non-compete or non-solicitation agreement from a prior employer
- There is a "silent co-founder" who provided capital or IP and is not on the cap table

**Why:** Equity disputes that begin informally and are later memorialized incorrectly produce litigation. A $1,500–$3,000 attorney review at formation prevents $50K–$200K disputes later.

**Action:** Engage an attorney to facilitate the co-founder agreement before any equity is issued.

---

### 4. SAFE notes or priced equity at formation

If the founder intends to:
- Issue a SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) to an investor at or before formation
- Issue convertible notes at formation
- Accept money from any investor before the equity structure is finalized

**Why:** SAFEs issued before formation documents are complete create ambiguous ownership. Post-money SAFEs (YC's current standard) interact with the cap table in specific ways that require correct setup from day one.

**Action:** Use Clerky (which has native SAFE tooling) or engage an attorney to review the SAFE documentation before issuing.

---

### 5. Accelerated vesting disputes at formation

If co-founders are negotiating:
- Single-trigger vs double-trigger acceleration on acquisition
- Partial acceleration clauses
- Clawback provisions for misconduct
- Any deviation from the standard 4-year/1-year cliff

**Why:** Non-standard vesting provisions need careful drafting to be enforceable and to satisfy future investors.

**Action:** Clerky handles standard YC vesting terms. Any deviation requires attorney review.

---

### 6. Regulated industries

If the company operates in:
- Healthcare (HIPAA, FDA)
- Financial services (SEC, FINRA, money transmission)
- Cannabis (state licensing)
- Defense / dual-use technology (ITAR, EAR)
- Real estate (broker-dealer licensing)

**Why:** Regulated industries have licensing requirements that precede or constrain entity formation choices.

**Action:** Engage a specialist attorney for the regulated domain before forming the entity.

---

## What does NOT require an attorney

The following are routinely handled DIY via formation platforms or with this stinger's guidance:

- Delaware C-Corp formation for a US founding team
- Standard 4-year/1-year cliff vesting for up to 3 co-founders
- 83(b) election filing (electronic via Form 15620 or paper)
- EIN acquisition
- Business bank account opening
- Standard IP assignment (PIIA) via Clerky or Atlas templates
- Pilot or Bench bookkeeping setup
