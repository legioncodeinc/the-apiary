## Stripe Atlas

**Fee:** $500 one-time + $100/year registered agent (year 2+). The $500 includes the Delaware state filing fee.

**What's included:**
- Delaware C-Corp or LLC formation
- EIN acquisition
- Automatic 83(b) election filing (for stock grants)
- $2,500 Stripe processing credits
- $50K+ in partner discounts (AWS, Notion, Brex, etc.)
- Mercury or Brex bank account introduction (same-day setup)

**Processing time:** 1–2 business days (expedited state filing included). Fastest of the four platforms.

**Best for:**
- Solo SaaS founder planning to process payments with Stripe
- Non-US founders who need speed
- Founders who want the Amazon/Stripe partner discount stack
- Bootstrapped or VC-backed founders who want a no-fuss setup

**Not ideal for:**
- High SAFE activity or complex equity structures (Clerky is better)
- Founders who don't use or plan to use Stripe payments

Source: `research/external/stripe-atlas-official-docs-2026.md`

## Clerky

**Fee:** $425 formation (pay-per-use) OR $799 Lifetime package + $99/year Library subscription.

**What's included:**
- Delaware C-Corp formation (C-Corp only; no LLC)
- SAFE agreements (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) — the only platform with native SAFE tooling
- Founder stock purchase agreements with standard YC vesting (4-year, 1-year cliff)
- 83(b) election filing assistance
- IP assignment agreements
- Ongoing document library for YC-standard equity docs

**Best for:**
- YC-track startups or founders following YC deal terms
- Teams with heavy SAFE activity (seed round via SAFEs)
- Co-founders who need standard equity agreements with legal review baked in
- Founders who want the complete legal document stack, not just formation

**Not ideal for:**
- Solo founders with simple needs (Atlas is cheaper and faster)
- International founders needing an LLC
- Founders who don't anticipate SAFE-based fundraising

Source: `research/external/stripe-atlas-vs-clerky-comparison-2026.md`

## Doola

**Fee:** $297–$597 formation + $300–$500/year maintenance (or $1,999 Total Compliance annual package).

**What's included:**
- Delaware C-Corp OR Delaware LLC (confirmed C-Corp support in 2026)
- EIN acquisition
- Registered agent (included in annual fee)
- ITIN support for international founders (unique feature)
- US business address
- Compliance reminders

**Best for:**
- International founders who need the lowest entry price
- Non-US founders who need ITIN support alongside EIN
- Founders who want a managed compliance dashboard
- LLC formations (more flexible operating agreement tooling than Atlas)

**Not ideal for:**
- US founders who need speed (Atlas is faster)
- YC-track founders who need SAFE tooling (Clerky is better)

Source: `research/external/doola-firstbase-comparison-2026.md`

## Firstbase

**Fee:** $399–$599 formation + $299/year.

**What's included:**
- Delaware or Wyoming C-Corp or LLC
- EIN acquisition
- Registered agent (included)
- Compliance dashboard with deadline reminders
- US business address and virtual mailbox

**Best for:**
- Non-US founders who want a compliance dashboard
- Founders considering Wyoming as an alternative to Delaware
- Bootstrapped founders who want ongoing compliance tracking

**Not ideal for:**
- US founders (Atlas or Clerky are better-established options)
- SAFE-heavy equity structures (Clerky is better)

Source: `research/external/doola-firstbase-comparison-2026.md`

## Decision matrix

| Profile | Recommended platform | Why |
|---|---|---|
| US solo SaaS founder, VC-backed intent | **Stripe Atlas** | Speed, Stripe credits, Mercury intro |
| US founding team, YC-track, SAFE fundraising | **Clerky** | Native SAFE tooling, equity agreements |

## What all platforms include (table stakes)

All four platforms handle:
1. State of Delaware Articles of Incorporation filing
2. EIN acquisition (or guidance)
3. Registered agent for year 1 (Stripe Atlas at $100/year; others vary)
4. Basic organizational minutes / board consent

**What they do NOT handle:**
- Cap table management (Carta, Pulley — separate tool)
- Ongoing state franchise tax filings after formation
- Complex IP ownership analysis
- International holding structures
