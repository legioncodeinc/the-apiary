# Example: Happy Path — US Solo SaaS Founder

**Scenario:** US-resident solo founder, planning to raise VC seed, solo (no co-founders at formation), building a SaaS product on Stripe payments.

*Demonstrates: `guides/00-entity-type-decision.md`, `guides/01-formation-platforms.md`, `guides/02-ein-workflow.md`, `guides/03-banking.md`, `guides/04-bookkeeping.md`, `guides/05-founder-paperwork.md`*

---

## Founder profile (Bee input)

- Target use case: VC-backed SaaS
- Timeline: Urgent (< 1 week)
- Team: Solo founder, US citizen
- Budget: $500–$700 formation
- Revenue: $0 (pre-revenue)

---

## Step 1: Entity-type decision

**Bee output:**

> Qualifying question 1: "Do you intend to raise VC funding?" → Yes.
>
> Recommendation: **Delaware C-Corp**. 89% of US VC-backed companies use this structure. QSBS eligibility provides up to $10M federal capital gains exclusion (5-year hold). Annual cost: $450/year (Assumed Par Value Capital Method — always use this on the annual report, NOT the Authorized Shares Method which can generate a $76K+ tax bill).

---

## Step 2: Formation platform

**Bee output:**

> Platform recommendation: **Stripe Atlas**.
>
> Rationale: You are a solo founder using Stripe payments, and Atlas includes a $2,500 Stripe credit, EIN acquisition, auto-83(b) election filing, and a Mercury bank account introduction — all for $500. Processing time: 1–2 business days. Annual RA fee in year 2: $100.

---

## Step 3: EIN

**Bee output:**

> Atlas will handle EIN acquisition as part of the formation flow. No separate IRS application needed. The EIN will be available within 1–2 business days.
>
> Save the IRS CP 575 confirmation letter when it arrives — banks and investors will ask for it.

---

## Step 4: Banking

**Bee output:**

> Banking recommendation: **Mercury** (introduced via Atlas at no cost).
>
> Features relevant to you: No minimum balance, no monthly fees, $5M FDIC pass-through, Mercury Treasury for yield on cash balances. Open immediately after EIN confirmation via the Atlas bank intro link.
>
> Warning: Not applicable for your profile (US founder, non-sanctioned country).

---

## Step 5: Bookkeeping

**Bee output:**

> Bookkeeping recommendation: **DIY with QuickBooks Online ($30/month)** until monthly expenses exceed $25K–$50K, then transition to **Pilot Core ($499/month)** when preparing for Series A.
>
> Connect QuickBooks to your Mercury bank feed via OAuth. Set the accounting start date to your entity formation date.

---

## Step 6: Founder paperwork

**Bee output:**

> Stripe Atlas handles the following automatically:
> - Stock purchase agreement (founder stock at $0.0001/share)
> - 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff (standard)
> - 83(b) election filing (Atlas auto-files on your behalf)
> - Initial board consent and organizational minutes
>
> **Action required from you:**
> - Sign the IP assignment agreement (PIIA) in the Atlas document flow.
> - Verify with Atlas that the 83(b) election was filed and retain the confirmation.
>
> **83(b) deadline:** Atlas handles this automatically, but verify the filing date in the Atlas dashboard within 48 hours of stock issuance. The 30-day window is absolute.
