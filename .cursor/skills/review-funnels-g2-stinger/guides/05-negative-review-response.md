# Negative Review Response: review-funnels-g2-stinger

## The respond-always principle

Every negative review deserves a response. Unanswered negative reviews lower conversion more than the negative review itself -- buyers infer that the vendor doesn't care, or that the issue is systemic.

**48-hour SLA:** Respond within 48 hours of a review appearing. G2 and Trustpilot both surface "last responded" timestamps.

Source: `research/external/2026-05-20-negative-review-response-strategy.md`

---

## The Acknowledge / Clarify / Resolve / Close framework

Every negative review response follows this four-part structure:

### 1. Acknowledge (1-2 sentences)
- Thank the reviewer for taking the time.
- Acknowledge the specific frustration -- by name, not generically.
- Do NOT use "I'm sorry you feel that way" (dismissive) or "We're sorry for any inconvenience" (too generic).

### 2. Clarify (1-2 sentences, when applicable)
- If there is a factual misrepresentation, correct it once -- politely, with evidence if possible.
- If the complaint is valid, skip the clarify step entirely; do not defend.
- Never argue. "We respectfully disagree" is the ceiling of pushback.

### 3. Resolve (1-2 sentences)
- State the specific action you're taking or have taken to address the issue.
- Offer to take the conversation offline: "Please reach out to [email] or [support channel] so we can make this right."
- If the issue is a known bug or improvement: "This is on our roadmap and we expect to address it by [Q/timeframe]."

### 4. Close (1 sentence)
- Express genuine appreciation for the feedback.
- Optional: invite them to revisit their review if the issue is resolved.
- Do NOT beg for a review update (some platforms prohibit requesting review updates directly).

## Response templates by star-rating band

### 1-star response (customer experienced a significant failure)

```
Hi [Name or "there"],

Thank you for taking the time to share this feedback. I can see this experience was genuinely frustrating, and I want to address it directly.

[Specific acknowledgment of their issue.]

[What we've done or are doing: e.g., "I've reviewed your account and can see [specific issue]. I've escalated this to our [team] and we'd like to resolve it with you directly."]

Please reach out to me at [email] — I want to make sure we turn this around for you.

[Your name], [Title]
[Company]
```

### 2-star response (significant but partial failure)

```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for your honest feedback. I can see [specific issue] impacted your experience significantly, and that's not the level of service we aim for.

[Brief clarification if warranted, or skip.] 

[Specific resolution offer or action taken.]

I'd love the opportunity to discuss this directly — [email/support channel]. We appreciate customers who take the time to tell us when we're falling short.

[Your name]
```

### 3-star response (mixed experience)

```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for the balanced feedback. It's clear you've found [positive aspect] valuable, and we hear you on [specific negative aspect].

[What we're doing about the negative aspect: roadmap item, recent fix, or workaround.]

We'd love to earn a higher rating once [improvement]. If you'd like to share more, I'm at [email].

[Your name]
```

### 4-star response (positive but with a specific gap)

```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for the great feedback and for noting [specific gap].

[One sentence on what we're doing to close that gap.]

We'd love to hear more about your experience anytime — [email/support].

[Your name]
```

## Decision tree: when to escalate vs. respond directly

```
New negative review appears
        |
        v
Is the review flagging a security/legal/compliance issue?
  YES → Escalate to legal/security team before responding.
  NO  → Continue
        |
        v
Does the review contain false factual claims (provable)?
  YES → Correct once politely with evidence. Do not argue further.
  NO  → Skip clarify step; go straight to resolve.
        |
        v
Is the review suspicious (same-day, no other reviews, vague)?
  YES → Flag to platform for policy review before responding.
  NO  → Respond per framework above.
        |
        v
Is this an enterprise customer or a high-ACV account?
  YES → Loop in CSM or account owner to reach out personally in addition to public response.
  NO  → Standard response.
```

## What NOT to do in a negative review response

- Do not copy-paste the same response across multiple reviews.
- Do not tag the reviewer by full name if they used a first-name-only handle.
- Do not offer discounts or gifts in the public response (looks like buying silence; also a potential FTC issue).
- Do not ask the reviewer to change their rating in the public response (G2 policy).
- Do not go silent for more than 48 hours.
