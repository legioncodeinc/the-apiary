# 03 — Facilitation Playbook

*Derived from `research/external/2026-05-20-sprint-retrospective-formats-comprehensive.md` and `research/external/2026-05-20-format-selection-meetgeek.md`.*

---

## The standard retro agenda

Total time: 60-90 minutes (standard sprint). 45 minutes for 1-week sprints.

| Segment | Time | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| Opening / check-in | 5 min | Icebreaker + ESVP check (Explorer / Shopper / Vacationer / Prisoner) |
| Previous actions review | 5-10 min | Score each prior action item: Done / In Progress / Dropped |
| Safety check (if new team) | 5 min | Anonymous Edmondson mini-survey or thumbs check |
| Brainstorm (silent) | 10-15 min | Each person writes cards independently — no discussion yet |
| Share and cluster | 10-15 min | Read cards aloud, group into themes (affinity mapping) |
| Vote on themes | 5 min | Dot voting: 3-5 dots per person on highest-priority themes |
| Discuss top themes | 15-20 min | Go deep on the top 2-3 voted themes — ask "why?" at least once |
| Action items | 10 min | Apply the 3-question filter (who, when, done-looks-like) to each proposed action |
| Closing ritual | 5 min | One word round + appreciation shout-out |

Adjust time per segment based on team size. Fewer participants = compress brainstorm and clustering; more = compress closing ritual.

---

## Opening: the ESVP check

Ask each participant to identify which role they're in today (silently; show hands or write on a sticky):

- **Explorer:** Genuinely curious, open to discovery.
- **Shopper:** Hoping to find one or two useful things.
- **Vacationer:** Happy to be away from work, not expecting much.
- **Prisoner:** Here because they have to be.

Do not use this to shame Vacationers and Prisoners. Use it to calibrate energy management. If most of the room is Vacationers or Prisoners, acknowledge it aloud and ask what would make the session worth their time. The check-in that surfaces low buy-in is more valuable than the one that masks it.

---

## Icebreakers (select one)

Keep icebreakers under 3 minutes. Options:

- **Weather report:** Each person names their mood as weather (sunny, cloudy, stormy). Fast and neutral.
- **One word:** Each person says one word that describes the sprint. Forces concision.
- **Best thing / challenge:** Each person names the best thing that happened this sprint and the biggest challenge. Doubles as a warm-up for the brainstorm.

Avoid icebreakers that require vulnerability until the team has built trust (skip "share something personal" for low-safety teams).

---

## Silent brainstorm (the most important facilitation rule)

Always brainstorm in silence before discussion. This is the single most impactful facilitation choice:

- Prevents anchor bias (early contributions dominate the board).
- Gives introverts time to formulate thoughts without being interrupted.
- Produces 2-3x more distinct items than open brainstorming.

Enforce silence actively. If someone starts talking during the brainstorm window, redirect gently: "Let's get all thoughts on the board first."

**Time-box the silent brainstorm:** 8-12 minutes. Use a visible timer.

---

## Affinity clustering

After the brainstorm, read each item aloud (the author reads their own, or the facilitator reads anonymously). Group similar items into clusters. Name the clusters.

Rules:
- No debate during clustering. If two items seem different, create separate clusters or ask the author which cluster fits better.
- Clusters should have names that capture the theme, not just the first item in the cluster.
- Aim for 5-8 clusters from a typical team brainstorm. More than 10 means the clustering wasn't specific enough.

---

## Dot voting

Give each participant 3-5 dots (use stickers, or digital tools' vote function). Each person places dots on the clusters or items they most want to discuss.

- 2-minute time limit.
- Participants may put multiple dots on one item.
- After voting, rank clusters by vote count. Discuss top 2-3 only.

Dot voting is fast, visual, democratic, and prevents the loudest voice from dominating the agenda.

Alternative for high-stakes decisions: **Fist-to-five** (each person shows 0-5 fingers on a topic, 0 = block, 5 = full support). Use this for action-item priority when dot voting feels insufficient.

---

## Discussion: "ask why at least once"

When discussing a top-voted cluster, the facilitator's most important move is to ask "why?" at least once. Not as an interrogation — as genuine curiosity.

"We keep missing estimates. Why?"
"We don't have enough context on stories before sprint start."
"Why don't you have enough context?"
"The acceptance criteria aren't written until day 2 of the sprint."

That's the level where action items become useful. "Improve estimates" is not an action. "Product owner writes acceptance criteria before sprint planning" is an action.

---

## Action item capture

Apply the 3-question filter to every proposed action before it leaves the discussion:
1. Who owns this? (One name.)
2. When does it close? (Specific date or "by next retro".)
3. What does done look like? (Observable outcome.)

Move confirmed action items directly into the sprint backlog or a designated tracking board. Action items that don't make it into the backlog have a near-zero completion rate.

See `guides/04-action-items.md` for the full discipline.

---

## Closing ritual

End every retro with two things:

1. **One-word round:** Each participant says one word describing how they feel leaving the retro. This is a quick emotional pulse and a natural close.
2. **Appreciation:** One round of explicit appreciations (optional but high-value for team morale). "I want to appreciate [name] for [specific thing]." Keep it concrete — "thanks for being great" is not an appreciation.

---

## Facilitation failure modes to avoid

- **Skipping the silent brainstorm.** Open brainstorming produces anchor bias. Silent is non-negotiable.
- **Allowing discussion during clustering.** This is a phase-ordering error. Cluster first, discuss after vote.
- **Discussing more than 2-3 themes.** Teams that try to cover all themes produce shallow discussion and weak action items. Vote and cut.
- **Leaving action items unassigned.** An action item with no owner is a wish, not a commitment.
- **Running over time.** Time-box each segment visibly. The closing ritual is not optional — it communicates that the team's time and energy are respected.
