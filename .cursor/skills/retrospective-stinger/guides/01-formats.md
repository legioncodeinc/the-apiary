# 01 — Format Matrix

*Derived from `research/external/2026-05-20-sprint-retrospective-formats-comprehensive.md` and `research/external/2026-05-20-format-selection-meetgeek.md`.*

---

## Format selection logic

Choose based on: (1) team maturity, (2) period valence, (3) time budget, (4) remote/sync. The table below gives the signal for each.

**Rotation cadence:** Rotate formats every 2-4 sprints to prevent staleness. Introduce a new format when energy has dropped or the team is recycling the same complaints.

---

## The nine canonical formats

### 1. Start / Stop / Continue (SSC)

**Structure:** Three columns. What should we start doing? What should we stop doing? What should we continue doing?
**Best for:** Teams new to retrospectives, any team after a long period without retros, teams wanting a quick reset. Universally understood; low facilitation overhead.
**Time budget:** 45-60 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Low.
**Voting:** Dot voting on top items in each column.
**When NOT to use:** Mature teams who have been using SSC for more than 4 consecutive sprints (staleness kills engagement). High-conflict periods where "stop doing" becomes a blame column.

---

### 2. 4Ls (Liked / Learned / Lacked / Longed For)

**Structure:** Four columns focused on learning and value.
**Best for:** Teams after a significant learning event (onboarding, new tech, major delivery). Good for teams where knowledge-sharing is a cultural value. Strong async format.
**Time budget:** 45-60 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Low-medium.
**Voting:** Dot voting or fist-to-five on Lacked and Longed For items.
**When NOT to use:** Teams in a low-energy or high-conflict period (the format is optimistic by design and can feel tone-deaf).

---

### 3. Sailboat

**Structure:** Wind (what's accelerating us?), Anchor (what's slowing us down?), Rocks (risks ahead), Sun (the goal/vision).
**Best for:** Mid-sprint check-ins, teams facing external blockers, teams that benefit from visualizing impediments vs. enablers.
**Time budget:** 60-75 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Medium.
**Voting:** Dot voting on anchors and rocks (highest priority impediments to address).
**When NOT to use:** Short sprints (1 week) where the metaphor overhead isn't worth it.

---

### 4. Mad / Sad / Glad

**Structure:** Three emotional quadrants. What made you mad (frustrated)? What made you sad (disappointed or demotivated)? What made you glad (energized or proud)?
**Best for:** Emotionally difficult periods (incident, team conflict, missed deadline, project cancellation). Good for surfacing morale issues a process-focused format would miss.
**Time budget:** 60-75 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Medium-high (requires facilitation skill to avoid devolution into complaint session).
**Voting:** Affinity grouping before voting; focus action items on Mad items.
**When NOT to use:** Teams with low psychological safety (emotional formats require more trust, not less). Do NOT use when a team hasn't built a baseline of psychological safety — run a safety exercise first.

---

### 5. DAKI (Drop / Add / Keep / Improve)

**Structure:** Four quadrants focused on practices: what to drop entirely, add new, keep as-is, and improve.
**Best for:** Mature teams wanting to refine their own process. Good after a team has been running SSC for several sprints and wants more granularity.
**Time budget:** 60-75 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Medium.
**Voting:** Dot voting on Drop and Improve items (highest-priority changes).
**When NOT to use:** New teams (too abstract without reference to established practices).

---

### 6. Starfish

**Structure:** Five areas: Stop, Less of, Keep, More of, Start.
**Best for:** Teams wanting more nuance than SSC's binary (stop vs. start). Good for teams where "stop" feels too drastic but "less of" captures the intent better.
**Time budget:** 60-75 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Medium.
**Voting:** Dot voting across all five categories.
**When NOT to use:** Teams unfamiliar with the format (brief intro needed); time-constrained sessions.

---

### 7. Hot Air Balloon (Sky/Sandbags)

**Structure:** What's helping us fly high (tailwinds/hot air)? What's weighing us down (sandbags)?
**Best for:** Teams that benefit from forward-looking framing over backward-looking complaint. Good for teams after a difficult sprint who need to maintain morale.
**Time budget:** 45-60 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Low.
**Voting:** Dot voting on sandbags.
**When NOT to use:** Mature teams who find the metaphor patronizing.

---

### 8. 5 Whys Retrospective

**Structure:** Identify one significant problem or outcome from the sprint. Ask "Why?" five times to reach the root cause. Then identify the action that addresses the root, not the symptom.
**Best for:** Post-incident retrospectives, recurring problems that SSC has failed to fix, teams that keep generating the same action items sprint over sprint.
**Time budget:** 45-60 minutes per problem (limit to 1-2 problems).
**Facilitation complexity:** High (facilitator must resist the pull toward symptoms and keep drilling to root).
**Voting:** Select the 1-2 problems to investigate via dot vote before starting.
**When NOT to use:** General sprint review (too narrow for broad feedback); teams with low psychological safety (root causes often involve people or culture).

---

### 9. Learning Matrix

**Structure:** Four quadrants: What went well? What could be improved? Ideas to try? Appreciations (shout-outs).
**Best for:** Teams that want to combine the positive energy of a "went well" review with concrete improvement ideas and explicit appreciation. The appreciation quadrant is distinctive and good for morale.
**Time budget:** 60-75 minutes.
**Facilitation complexity:** Low-medium.
**Voting:** Dot voting on "could be improved" and "ideas to try".
**When NOT to use:** Teams where the appreciation quadrant would feel forced or performative.

Note: Some practitioners use "Learning Matrix" as an alternate name for "4Ls". Clarify with the team if confusion arises. See `research/research-summary.md` open question #5.

## Quick-reference selection matrix

| Context | Recommended format | Fallback |
|---|---|---|
| New team / first retro | Start/Stop/Continue | Hot Air Balloon |
| Learning-heavy sprint | 4Ls | Learning Matrix |
| High conflict or morale issue | Mad/Sad/Glad | Sailboat |
| Recurring problems | 5 Whys | DAKI |
| External blockers | Sailboat | Hot Air Balloon |
| Process maturity / refinement | DAKI | Starfish |
| High energy, want nuance | Starfish | DAKI |
| Post-incident | 5 Whys | Mad/Sad/Glad |
| Async team | 4Ls or Start/Stop/Continue | Sailboat |
