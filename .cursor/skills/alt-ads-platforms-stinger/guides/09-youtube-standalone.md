# 09 — YouTube Ads (Standalone Video)

This guide covers YouTube Ads when run as a **standalone video advertising channel**, separate from Google Ads search campaigns. YouTube's unique value is high-intent video engagement at scale, with audience targeting that leverages Google's first-party data.

Best-fit use case: brand awareness, product explainer campaigns, retargeting, D2C with visual product demonstration.

---

## When to run YouTube outside of Google Ads

Most advertisers run YouTube campaigns inside Google Ads (under the Videos campaign type). Running YouTube as a standalone channel means:
- Dedicated YouTube-specific budget with its own optimization goals (video views, brand lift, not search conversions).
- Separate creative testing cadence (video creative vs text/image creative).
- Brand awareness KPIs (view-through rate, reach, frequency) rather than CPL.

**Run YouTube as a standalone channel when:**
1. Brand awareness is an explicit campaign objective alongside direct response.
2. Budget is sufficient to run both video and search channels without cannibalizing.
3. The product has strong visual demonstration value (software UI walkthroughs, physical products, cooking/how-to content).

**Run YouTube inside Google Ads when:**
- Budget requires unified management.
- Conversion goals dominate awareness goals.
- No dedicated video creative team.

## Ad formats

**TrueView In-Stream (skippable):** Plays before or during YouTube videos. Viewers can skip after 5 seconds. You pay only when viewers watch 30+ seconds or click. Best for brand awareness with targeted messaging.

**TrueView for Action (non-skippable equivalent with CTA):** Combines skippable video with a prominent call-to-action overlay. Best for direct response.

**Non-Skippable In-Stream (6-15 seconds):** Unskippable bumper-length ads. Best for brand awareness and message reinforcement. Cannot be used for direct response.

**Bumper Ads (6 seconds, non-skippable):** Pure brand awareness. Extremely short; effective for retargeting sequences.

**YouTube Shorts Ads:** Vertical 9:16 format, 6-60 seconds. Inserted between Shorts in the Shorts feed. Growing significantly as Shorts adoption increases in 2025-2026.

**In-Feed Video Ads (formerly Discovery Ads):** Appears in YouTube search results, recommendations, and homepage. User must click to watch. Best for content discovery and channel growth.

## Audience targeting on YouTube

YouTube inherits Google's first-party audience data — the most comprehensive in the advertising industry.

**High-value targeting options:**
- **Customer Match:** Upload your customer email list; target or exclude existing customers.
- **In-Market audiences:** Users actively researching products in your category.
- **Life events:** Users experiencing specific life transitions (moving, getting married, graduating).
- **Custom intent audiences:** Build audiences based on users who searched specific keywords on Google.
- **Affinity audiences:** Broad interest-based audiences for awareness campaigns.
- **YouTube channel remarketing:** Target users who watched your channel's videos.

## Creative benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Cost per view (TrueView) | $0.01-$0.03 |
| CPM (non-skippable/bumper) | $5-$30 |
| View-through rate (skippable) | 20-35% |
| YouTube Shorts completion rate | 40-60% |

**Creative best practices:**
- Hook in the first 5 seconds (before the skip button) — your message must be clear before viewers skip.
- For TrueView, the first 5 seconds should state who the ad is for ("If you're a DevOps engineer...").
- Include a verbal and visual CTA at the end.
- 15-30 second ad length outperforms 60+ seconds for direct response on YouTube.

---

## Conversion tracking

YouTube conversions are tracked via Google Tag Manager (GTM) or Google Ads conversion tag. View-through conversions (user saw the ad but did not click, then converted later) are tracked with a default 3-day window — adjust this window based on your sales cycle length.

For brand awareness campaigns, use YouTube Brand Lift studies (available in Google Ads for campaigns over $50K) to measure recall, consideration, and favorability lift.
