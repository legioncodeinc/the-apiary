# Ohio Law Citations for ADA/DevPipe Forensic Cases

Statutory and caselaw authority used in the Attorney Memo and Demand Letters. Cuyahoga County, Ohio is the primary venue.

## Statutory Authority

### Ohio Consumer Sales Practices Act (CSPA) — Ohio Rev. Code § 1345.01 et seq.
- Prohibits unfair, deceptive, or unconscionable acts in connection with consumer transactions.
- **Treble damages** plus **attorney's fees** for knowing violations (§ 1345.09).
- May apply where the misrepresented service involves a "personal" or "family" element.
- Counsel should evaluate viability where the client is an individual operating a small business in her name.

### Ohio Fraud — Common Law
Elements (Ohio):
1. A material false representation made
2. With knowledge of its falsity (or with reckless disregard for the truth)
3. With intent to induce reliance
4. Which the plaintiff justifiably relied upon
5. Resulting in damages

**Punitive damages:** Ohio law permits punitive damages for fraud (Ohio Rev. Code § 2315.21), capped at twice the compensatory damages or $350,000, whichever is greater (with additional caps for corporate defendants).

### Ohio Breach of Contract — Common Law
Elements:
1. Existence of a valid contract
2. Performance by the plaintiff
3. Breach by the defendant
4. Damages

### Ohio Gross Negligence
Requires conduct manifesting reckless disregard for the rights of others, beyond mere negligence. Available where a defendant's conduct is sufficiently egregious — typically pierces contractual limitation-of-liability clauses.

### Extortion — Ohio Rev. Code § 2905.11
**CAUTION FOR THE CLIENT:** Do NOT make demands accompanied by threats of:
- Criminal prosecution
- Publicizing information ("we'll tell the press")
- Damaging reputation outside of court records

Each of these can constitute extortion under Ohio Rev. Code § 2905.11, exposing the demanding party to criminal liability. Legitimate threats of civil litigation are fine.

## Liability Cap — Why ADA's TOS Does Not Apply for Fraud

ADA's Terms of Service contains:
> "the entire liability of Acme Digital Agency and any of its suppliers under any provision of this Terms and Your exclusive remedy for all of the foregoing shall be limited to the amount actually paid by You through the Service or 100 USD if You haven't purchased anything through the Service."

Under Ohio law, contractual liability caps generally DO NOT enforce against:
1. **Intentional fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation** — A party cannot contract away its own intentional fraud.
2. **Gross negligence** — Most Ohio courts will not enforce caps for grossly negligent conduct, especially in commercial contexts involving consumer-protection violations.
3. **Statutory damages under CSPA** — The CSPA's treble damages and attorney's fees are not waivable by contract.

Cite these principles in the Attorney Memo Section III.A and the Demand Letter Section II.

## Personal Liability / Veil-Piercing — Ohio

To pierce the corporate veil and reach Sameer Khan personally (or Robert Hartwell personally), establish:
1. **Domination of the entity** — The principal exercises complete control such that the entity has no separate mind, will, or existence.
2. **Wrongful use of that control** — Used to perpetuate fraud, evade obligations, or commit some other illegal act.
3. **Resulting harm** — The unjust loss or injury suffered by the plaintiff.

For Offshore Build/DevPipe, the factors that support veil-piercing:
- Pakistan-based operation with US-shell incorporation
- Minimal US assets
- Use of the entity to perpetuate the maintenance-fraud pattern
- No board minutes, no corporate formalities

For Robert Hartwell / ADA, the factors are weaker (Northstar Holdings has its own existence and Ohio assets), but the same internal-domination argument applies.

## Spoliation Sanctions

Ohio law treats willful destruction of evidence as sanctionable conduct. The litigation hold notices in the Demand Letters reference this. Sanctions can include:
- Adverse-inference jury instruction
- Striking pleadings
- Dismissal of defenses
- Monetary sanctions
- Default judgment

Each demand letter and findings notice must include explicit litigation-hold language to enable these sanctions if the defendants destroy evidence after notice.

## Treble Damages Under CSPA

Where Ohio CSPA applies (§ 1345.09):
- Plaintiff may elect treble damages OR statutory damages ($200 per violation), whichever is greater
- Plaintiff may recover attorney's fees if defendant knew or should have known the conduct violated CSPA
- The cap does not apply to non-economic damages

For a Example Booking Co.-style case with $173,000–$362,500 in actual damages:
- Treble damages alone = $519,000–$1,087,500
- Plus attorney's fees

CSPA is the strongest legal lever where it applies. Counsel should evaluate viability for each case.
