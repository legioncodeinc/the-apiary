# Findings Notice — Template

Pre-demand letter establishing the evidentiary record. Sent BEFORE the demand letter to create a paper trail.

## Header (same as demand letter, but subject is different)

```
Re: Notice of Forensic Findings — {CLIENT_LEGAL_NAME} Engagement ({START} – {END})
    Reference: {CONTRACT_REFERENCE}
```

## Body Structure

1. **Introduction** — Forensic investigation has been completed. ~90 pages of formal reporting + 51-tab Excel + 152 emails + 8 contract docs. Full evidence packet preserved.

2. **Section I — Summary of Findings**
   - For ADA: Hosting double-billing, Virtual Assistant non-delivery, Social Media non-performance, Software-build failure (ADA as original contracting party)
   - For DevPipe: Software quality 3.1/10, recurring appointment time bomb, maintenance billing vs delivered effort, dependency maintenance not performed

3. **Section II — Evidence Available to Your Counsel**
   - Master Forensic Report
   - Master Invoice Spreadsheet with git log
   - 152 email messages
   - Audit reports
   - WordPress audit log (where applicable)
   - Avada changelog

4. **Section III — Notice of Litigation Hold**
   - Specific evidence categories to preserve
   - For ADA: VA timesheets, hosting records, Initial Build Vendor relationship, Offshore Build subcontracting
   - For DevPipe: timesheets, Stripe accounts, internal communications, git repository preservation

5. **Section IV — Next Steps**
   - A formal Demand Letter will follow within 7 calendar days
   - Recommendations: retain counsel, preserve evidence, cease further billing, direct communications through counsel
   - 7-day window to engage in pre-litigation discussion

## Signature + Reservation of Rights

Same as demand letter.

## Tone Differences vs Demand Letter

The Findings Notice is INFORMATIONAL — laying out the record. The Demand Letter is the FORMAL DEMAND.

- Findings Notice: "We have completed an investigation. Here is what we found. A demand letter will follow."
- Demand Letter: "Pay $X within Y days or we sue."

Both share the litigation-hold language and the reservation-of-rights footer.
