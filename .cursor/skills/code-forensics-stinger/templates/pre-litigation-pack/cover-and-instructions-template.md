# Cover and Instructions — Template

Internal document. Tactical guidance for the client and counsel. NEVER sent to defendants.

## Structure

### Title page
- "Pre-Litigation Document Package"
- "{CLIENT_LEGAL_NAME} v. ADA / DevPipe"
- "Six-Document Pre-Litigation Pack"

### Section: Read Before Use
- Strongly recommend retained counsel review before sending
- Service by certified mail (in addition to email) is strongly recommended

### Section: Document Inventory and Recommended Sequence

| # | Document | Recipient | When to Send |
|---|---|---|---|
| 00 | Cover & Instructions | (Internal) | N/A |
| 01 | Findings Notice | ADA | Day 0 |
| 02 | Findings Notice | DevPipe | Day 0 |
| 03 | Demand Letter | ADA | Day 7 (14-day cure) |
| 04 | Demand Letter | DevPipe | Day 7 (21-day cure for intl) |
| 05 | Termination Notice | ADA | Day 0 or after demand expires |
| 06 | Termination Notice | DevPipe | Day 0 or after demand expires |

### Section: Strategy Notes

- Findings Notices establish a paper trail of pre-litigation discussion
- Demand Letters are the formal demands with deadlines
- Termination Notices formalize the actual termination date
- Why 14-day vs 21-day deadline split (domestic vs international)
- "Intimidating but legally safe" tone formula

### Section: After Service

- Track receipts (certified mail return receipts + email-delivery receipts)
- Do NOT engage in informal communication
- Watch for evidence destruction (spoliation alert)
- Block ongoing charges
- Engage counsel before cure deadlines expire

### Section: Recommended Counsel Profile

- Ohio commercial litigator (Cuyahoga County experience preferred)
- Experience in software disputes, breach of contract, Ohio CSPA
- Available for contingency or hybrid arrangement
- Willingness to subpoena offshore Stripe records and pursue veil-piercing
