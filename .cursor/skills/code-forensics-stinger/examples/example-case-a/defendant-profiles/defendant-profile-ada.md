## ADA Terms of Service (the limitation-of-liability defenses)

ADA's TOS contains aggressive defensive clauses. Per the analysis, these clauses generally do NOT survive fraud or gross-negligence claims under Ohio law, but they will be raised:

### Refund Policy
- **Web Design:** "Once a payment or deposit is made, it is non-refundable."
- **Other Projects:** "All payments to Acme Digital Agency are non-refundable."
- **Chargebacks:** $50 fee. Chargebacks for valid charges characterized as "fraud."

### Subscription Cancellation
- 30-day notice required for monthly subscription cancellation.
- Submitted in writing to support@ada.example.
- (DOES NOT APPLY to termination for cause / material breach.)

### Limitation of Liability
- Capped at the amount actually paid OR $100, whichever is less.
- Disclaims all special, incidental, indirect, or consequential damages.
- "AS IS" and "AS AVAILABLE" warranty disclaimer.

### Governing Law
- Ohio (good for the client — Ohio CSPA available; Cuyahoga County local venue)

## How to attack the ADA defensive posture

In every demand letter and complaint, address these defenses head-on:

1. **Limitation of Liability** — pierce on fraud and gross negligence (Ohio doesn't enforce caps for intentional fraud)
2. **Non-refundable payments** — restitution for services not rendered is not a refund; it's damages for breach
3. **30-day notice for cancellation** — inapplicable to termination for cause
4. **Chargeback fee** — the FCBA (Fair Credit Billing Act) protects chargebacks for services not actually rendered
