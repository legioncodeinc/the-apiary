These numbers give the Bee concrete thresholds to cite when advising on take-home design rather than expressing vague discomfort.

## 5 Open Questions for stinger-forge

This is notable because Ashby is the primary recommendation for data-driven tech teams, and LinkedIn Recruiter is ubiquitous at those teams. stinger-forge should either: (a) verify Ashby RSC support through current Ashby documentation before publishing guides/05, or (b) flag this as "verify with vendor" in the guide. Research did not surface a definitive answer.

### Q2: Rippling Recruiting D&I reporting depth
The research confirms Rippling Recruiting's HRIS integration advantage and its 2026 feature set, but Rippling's native D&I and EEOC funnel reporting capabilities are not documented in available public sources. The guides/03 ATS-native vs export comparison table needs a Rippling row - stinger-forge should check Rippling's current product documentation or mark it as "verify with vendor."

### Q3: Take-home compensation rate guidance
The research confirms the ethical framework (pay for assessments over ~2 hours) and quantifies the candidate experience impact, but does not surface a defensible compensation rate recommendation (e.g., "pay $X/hour" or "$Y stipend"). The Command Brief asks for "pay rate guidance" in guides/04. stinger-forge will need to apply practitioner judgment here or frame it as "equivalent to the role's hourly rate" as a principled but verifiable formula.

### Q4: Pinpoint and Lever RSC status
Lever is listed as an RSC partner in LinkedIn's help documentation; Pinpoint's RSC status is not confirmed in public documentation. If stinger-forge includes a RSC-compatibility row in the guides/00 platform comparison table, the Pinpoint cell needs a "verify with vendor" note.

### Q5: AEDT bias audit jurisdiction map
The research identifies that state-level Automated Employment Decision Tool (AEDT) bias audit requirements are emerging (NYC Local Law 144 is the pioneer) but does not map which jurisdictions have active requirements in 2026. guides/03 should acknowledge this as a rapidly evolving area and recommend users verify current jurisdiction requirements with legal counsel rather than relying on a static list in this stinger.

## Contradictions Between Sources
